SUMSRI Abstracts
2006

Research Seminars are the most important portion of the SUMSRI program.  Professional mathematicians and statisticians contribute problems and lead seminars. At least one of the seminar leaders is African American; the other two are Miami faculty from the Department of Mathematics and Statistics. Before arriving in Oxford, the students are mailed a card listing each seminar area and prerequisites. They are asked to rank the topics and return the card. They are then assigned an area based on their choice. We hope to give each person his or her first choice, but this is not always possible. During the first three weeks of the program, each seminar director presents a series of lectures to the seminar participants in their area of expertise and assign research problems. These problems are challenging and at the same time easy enough for a very good undergraduate student to get partial results. Each team chooses a problem to work on and consults the appropriate professional. The seminar leaders are asked to meet with their students every day except Friday during the first three weeks and at least twice a week during the last four weeks. We strongly encourage students to work in groups. At the end of the program, the students give an oral presentation on their results and write a paper.

"Reckless or Responsible: A Multivariate Statistical Analysis of Consumer Spending" by
Emilola Abayomi, Albany State University
Erin Esp, Montana Tech
and Shannon Grant, University of Idaho

As Americans spend more and save less, there is a need to evaluate variables which influence spending habits.  First, we reduce the number of variables with Principal Components analysis and identify underlying factors by grouping correlated variables in Factor Analysis.  Finally, we use Discriminant Analysis to develop a rule for classifying individual consumers as either reckless or responsible spenders.

"Education By Nation: A Multivariate Statistical Analysis"  by
Ashley Brooks, Winston-Salem State University
Amber Shoecraft, Johnson C. Smith University
and Anthony Franklin, Coastal Carolina University
 
We analyze education systems of 64 countries using multivariate statistical techniques such as principal component analysis, factor analysis, and discriminant analysis. Our goal is to classify countries into two populations, one where the educational system of the country is exceptional and the other where the educational system is fair. Reducing the dimensionality of the data set simplifies this process.

The Structure of Zero Divisor Sum Graphs by
Chantelle Bicket,  College of St. Mary
Samantha Graffeo, Swarthmore College
Whitney Ross, Spelman College
Edward Washington, Winston-Salem State University

Let ∑n be the graph whose vertex set is the set of non-zero zero divisors of Zn where vw is an edge if v+w is a non-zero divisor.  We study various graph-theoretic properties of ∑n including vertex degree, connectivity and cycles.  Further investigation is also made into planar graphs and automorphisms of these kinds of graphs.

Research Director:  Dr. Reza Akhtar, Miami University
Graduate Assistant:  Laura Lynch, University of Nebraska, Lincoln

"Elliptic Curves with Torsion Subgroup Z2 x Z8:  Does a Rank 4 Curve Exist?" 

by Terris Brooks, Central State University,
Elizabeth Fowler, Maryville College,
Katherine Hastings, Baldwin-Wallace College,
Danielle Hiance, Campbellsville University,
Matthew Zimmerman, Central State University

We consider elliptic curves over Q with torsion subgroup of Z2 x Z8. These curves are birationally equivalent to y2=(1-x2)(1-k2 x2) where k=(t4-6t2+1)/(t2+1)2 for some rational number t.  The largest known rank for such curves is 3.   In this paper we search for a curve of rank at least 4 by computing ranks for t=a/b with |a|,|b| <=2000.

Research Director:  Dr. Edray Goins, Purdue University
Research Assistant:  Holly Attenborough, Miami University/Indiana University

Matthew Zimmerman, Danielle Hiance, and Elizabeth Fowler
