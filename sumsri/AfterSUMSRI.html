and Miami University		 After 12 years of SUMSRI and 172 participants, we see many successes in many fields of mathematics and statistics and a few in other fields as well!

16 (9.3%) of former SUMSRI participants have successfully completed their PhDs.  6 of them are in business or government jobs.  The rest are professors teaching mathematics or statistics all across the country.  A couple of people are in business and teaching college classes in the evenings!

68 former SUMSRI participants (39.5%) have completed their masters degrees or passed their qualifying exams for the PhD.  Of those who are no longer students, they are working in business/insurance, government, teaching at junior colleges or high schools, or working for organizations such as the Mayo Clinic.

40 more (23.2%) have started masters degree programs.

Many of SUMSRI's former participants have taken on responsibilities for recruitment of a diverse mathematics and statistics student body, doing the background work for math and stat conferences, and more.

And there are 20 (11.6%) who are still undergraduates.

We have lost track of just 8 former SUMSRI participants.  

We enjoy hearing from former SUMSRI participants.  Many have married, had children, and shared their lives with us.  Facebook has proven itself to be a great venue for sharing pictures of children, weddings, graduations, etc.
